How to make medieval mead like a Viking


Take 1 gallon of fine honey

and to that 4 gallons of water

and heat that water till it be as "lengh"

then dissolve the honey in the water

then set them over the fire & let them boild and

ever scum it as long as any filth riseth there on

and then take it down off the fire

and let it cool in another vessel till it be as cold

as milk when it cometh from the cow

then take dregs of the finest ale or else barme

and cast it into the water & the honey

and stir all well together...

and so let it stand 3 days & nights...

Then Draw it from the dregs as clear as thou

may in to another vessel clean

& let it stand 1 night or 2

and then draw it into another vessel

and serve it forth.


Ingredients:

  • 1 gal of water
  • 1 quart of honey
  • 2 tbsp of ale dregs (barm) or 1/3 of a packet of dried ale yeast

Photo by Elena Leya on Unsplash


Steps:

  1. On a large pot hear the water until boiling
  2. Add the honey and mix until it's dissolved
  3. While it's heating skim off any scum
  4. Once boiled take it off the fire and pour it into a big vessel (you may need a funnel)
  5. Let the mixture cool until it's around your body temperature
  6. Then pour the dregs, barm of dried yeast
  7. After that cover the top not air tight
  8. And leave it be away from the sunlight, like in a closet
  9. By next day you should see tiny bubbles rising from the top
  10. Leaving it for more days will get a better color
  11. But after three days it's better to transfer it to another container
  12. Be careful of not transferring the dregs of yeast too
  13. And put the Bubbler Airlock on the cointainer (use a container will a small hole)
  14. Let it sit for some time from a couple of nights to six months
  15. Check it from time to time
  16. Once you think it's a good time pour it in a fitting glass and cheers!
